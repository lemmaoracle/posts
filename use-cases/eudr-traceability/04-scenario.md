---
title: "Scenario: palm oil into the EU under EUDR"
---


An EU food manufacturer imports refined palm oil through a Southeast Asian trader. The trader aggregates from several mills, each fed by smallholder cooperatives whose members hold the actual plot data. The 2026 EUDR cycle demands per-shipment due diligence: every plot referenced, every harvest date, every permit checked against the regulation's deforestation-free cutoff.

In the document-based workflow, each cooperative submits a self-attested PDF to its mill. The mill rolls them up. The trader rolls those up again. The importer files a due-diligence statement bound to a paperwork chain no one can independently verify, and any single producer-side leak — plot coordinates landing in a competitor's hands — undermines the cooperative's negotiating position for next season.

With per-attribute attestation, each producer issues a ZK proof: "harvest from plot in country X, after the EUDR reference date, under permit P." The cooperative composes its members' proofs into a single attestation, exposing only the aggregated attributes. The trader chains its own. The importer's filing is one cryptographic object, verifiable by the EU authority and the auditor — no plot data ever leaves the producer-side perimeter.

Commodity-specific schemas (coffee, cocoa, palm oil, soy, rubber, beef, timber), cooperative aggregation patterns, and the reference attestation flow live in the post-Discovery materials.
