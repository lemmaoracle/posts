---
title: "How Lemma approaches it"
---


At the moment an AI makes a decision, Lemma seals the model identifier, the docHash of the input attributes, the cryptographic signature of the applied guideline, and the attribution of the final output into a single ZK proof. Source data stays inside your perimeter. What crosses to the verifier is only the cryptographic fact of "when which model decided what, based on which evidence."

Whatever the model is updated to afterward, the structure of past decisions remains intact. When an appeal or audit arrives a year later, the regulator, the auditor, and the appellant can each verify the same proof independently — without your organization exposing the underlying data.

Whether this fits your AI governance pipeline is something we can confirm first through a conversation.
